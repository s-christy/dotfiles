Lindenmayer Systems

Lindenmayer systems (more succinctly written as "L-Systems") are formal language rules which have the capacity to generate arbitrarily complex strings given only an alphabet, a set of rules, and an axiom to apply those rules to. In formal math, the system is defined by the tuple G = (V, ω, P). V, ω, and P will be used in this text to refer to the alphabet, axiom, and rules of the system for the remainder of this text.

To illustrate this concept, assume the following:

V=AB

ω=A

P=AB.A

These rules are the original L-System described by Aristid Lindenmayer. This system will replace every occurrence of the character A with AB and every occurrence of B with A. Here is a simple demo to show the system in action:

Sierpinski's Triangle

L-Systems are interesting in their own right for linguistics and information theory because of their tendency to produce complex strings from simple rules, but they are more well known for graphical applications.

Consider these rules, when applied to an turtle graphics machine, this L-System will produce the Sierpinski Triangle.

V=FG+-

ω=F-G-G

P=F-G+F+G-F.GG.+.-

As you can see, as the system iterates itself, the triangle grows larger and more complicated. Via this process, we can quickly and efficiently build extremely complex systems.
